🗺️ Route Decision: Cologne or Bamberg?

We need to decide how we get from Bruges to Munich

Option A: Cologne → Bamberg → Munich

Cologne 2 nights — Kölsch brauhäuser, cathedral, Altstadt crawl.
Day trip or stopover in Bamberg en route to Munich (~3hr Cologne → Bamberg).
Düsseldorf add-on: Altbier city, only 30min from Cologne — could combine.

Option B: Skip Cologne → Bamberg 2–3 nights → Munich Recommended

Bamberg 2–3 nights — then 1hr train to Munich.
Maximizes time in the most beer-dense city on the planet per capita.
Still get Kölsch at any Belgian/German craft bar. Bamberg is irreplaceable.
Why Bamberg? A medieval UNESCO city of 70k people with 9+ breweries. Home of Rauchbier (smoked beer) — a style you can barely find anywhere else on earth. Schlenkerla tavern has been pouring smoked beer for 600 years — a genuine bucket-list beer experience. Serious beer nerds go out of their way for Bamberg. We're already in the neighbourhood.

💡 Düsseldorf note: If we do Cologne, Düsseldorf is 30min away — the Altbier capital. Could be a half-day side trip.

🎪 Oktoberfest Tent Strategy

Our targets — reservation windows matter

⚠️ OPEN NOW
Festzelt Tradition
Folk music only — the most traditional tent at Oktoberfest. Authentic Bavarian brass bands, no pop music. Smaller and incredibly charming.
Reservations OPEN — book immediately!
Opens July
Augustiner-Festhalle
The locals' favourite. The only tent still serving from traditional wooden barrels. Considered the best beer at Oktoberfest — you can taste the difference.
Reservations open July 2026
Opens mid-May
Fischer-Vroni
Fish specialty tent — Steckerlfisch (grilled fish on a stick) is legendary. Smaller and cozier than the big halls. Great for a change of pace.
Reservations open mid-May 2026
